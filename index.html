About Me

I was born in Georgia and later moved to Texas, where I earned my Bachelor of Arts in History from Angelo State University. I’ve been teaching for three years, developing a passion for helping students understand how history shapes the world around them.

Whether I’m teaching AP Human Geography or guiding students through complex historical narratives, my goal is to make learning relevant, accessible, and exciting. I strive to build a classroom environment where students feel supported, challenged, and curious.

Woody Guthrie and the Grand Coulee Dam

Published in The Santa Angela Review, Volume 2  |  Competencies: Historical research, academic writing, analysis

This article examines the cultural and political context surrounding Woody Guthrie’s work about the Grand Coulee Dam. It explores the intersection of music, government messaging, and American identity during a transformative period in U.S. history.
